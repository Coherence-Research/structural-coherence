## 1. Purpose

This policy governs use of names, marks, and designations associated with **Structural Coherence (SC)** and the **Structural Coherence — Anchor Specification (SC-AS)**.

Its purpose is to:
- Prevent public confusion about canonical identity, authorship, and stewardship
- Prevent false endorsement or certification claims
- Preserve the integrity of certification marks and program claims

This policy is separate from the text license (see SC-LICENSE-000001). A text license does not grant trademark or certification-mark rights.

---

## 2. Protected Designations (Names)

Coherence Research asserts rights (including common-law trademark rights where applicable) in the following designations as identifiers of source and stewardship:

- **Structural Coherence**
- **Structural Coherence — Anchor Specification**
- **SC-AS**
- **Coherence Research** (organization name)

Additional marks or logos may be designated in writing by Coherence Research.

---

## 3. Permitted Use (Nominative / Descriptive Reference)

**Nominative / descriptive reference is permitted, including in commercial contexts**, provided it is truthful and non-confusing.

Examples of permitted statements:
- "Compatible with SC-AS v1.0"
- "Implements SC-CORE-000001 (Issuance v38)"
- "Designed to conform to Structural Coherence — Anchor Specification (SC-AS)"

Permitted use conditions:
1. The reference MUST be accurate and version-pinned where applicable.
2. The reference MUST NOT imply endorsement, sponsorship, or certification by Coherence Research.
3. The reference MUST NOT use the protected designations as a product name, company name, or primary brand.
4. The reference MUST NOT use logos or stylized marks unless separately authorized.

---

## 4. Prohibited Use

The following are prohibited without explicit written permission from Coherence Research:

- Using "Structural Coherence", "SC-AS", or related designations as a brand, product name, or organization name in a way that creates confusion about source
- Representing a work as "official", "canonical", or "issued by Coherence Research" when it is not
- Any statement that implies endorsement, sponsorship, partnership, or affiliation that is not real
- Use of any logo, seal, or stylized mark that Coherence Research designates as protected

---

## 5. Certification Marks and Certification Claims

Certification marks (and related claims such as "Certified", "Certified Conformance", or equivalent program claims) are controlled separately from general nominative reference.

Rules:
1. **Certification marks may be used only by parties explicitly granted certification status in writing by Coherence Research.**
2. A party that truthfully implements SC-AS MAY describe compatibility (Section 3) but MUST NOT claim certification unless granted.
3. "Compatible with SC-AS" and "Certified" are distinct claims; compatibility does not imply certification.

---

## 6. Enforcement and Remedies

Coherence Research may request correction or removal of non-compliant uses and may pursue remedies available under applicable law to prevent confusion or misrepresentation.

---

For permissions (including logo use, certification-mark use, or special cases), contact Coherence Research through official channels published at coherenceresearch.com.
