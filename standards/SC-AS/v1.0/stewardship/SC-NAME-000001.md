## 1. Purpose

This policy defines a consistent naming system for the **Structural Coherence (SC)** standard, including the immutable specification layer, downstream artifacts, and certification terminology. It ensures clarity for implementers, reviewers, and the public.

---

## 2. Definitions (Normative)

### 2.1 Standard
**Structural Coherence (SC)** is the umbrella name of the standard.

### 2.2 Anchor Specification (AS)
The **Anchor Specification (AS)** is the authoritative, immutable, versioned root reference for the Structural Coherence standard.

**Canonical statement:**
> The Anchor Specification (AS) is immutable and versioned. Updates occur only through new versioned releases.

### 2.3 The Structural Coherence Spec (the "Spec")
The phrase **"Structural Coherence Spec"** refers to the **Structural Coherence — Anchor Specification (SC-AS)** as a bundle composed of:

- **Core Specification** (SC-CORE-…)
- **Axioms Specification** (SC-AXIOM-…)

Governance, program, and operational documents are not "the Spec" unless explicitly included in the AS bundle.

### 2.4 Downstream Artifacts
**Downstream Artifacts** are derived works and operational materials that reference an AS release/version without modifying or misrepresenting the AS.

Downstream Artifacts include:
- **Profiles**
- **Extensions**
- **Conformance Materials** (tests, suites, procedures)
- **Implementations**
- **Educational Materials** (guides, commentary, translations)

### 2.5 Profiles
**Profiles** are normative documents that constrain, specialize, or contextualize the AS for a defined domain, environment, or risk class.

Profiles MUST:
- reference the AS version they build upon (e.g., "References SC-AS vX.Y")
- not be represented as the AS itself

### 2.6 Extensions
**Extensions** are supplemental modules or addenda that add optional scope or capabilities beyond the AS while preserving traceable linkage to an AS version.

### 2.7 Implementations
**Implementations** are software, systems, processes, or tooling that claim to implement requirements of an AS and/or referenced Profiles/Extensions.

### 2.8 Conformance
**Conformance** is the factual relationship that an implementation meets the normative requirements of an AS (and referenced Profiles/Extensions).

### 2.9 Certified Conformance
**Certified Conformance** is a designation granted by Coherence Research under a published certification program to implementations that pass defined evaluation.

**Canonical statement:**
> Only implementations meeting the conformance program may claim Certified Conformance or use certification marks.

## 3. Naming Conventions (Normative)

### 3.1 Standard Name
- **Structural Coherence**
- Abbrev: **SC**

### 3.2 Anchor Specification Name
- **Structural Coherence — Anchor Specification (SC-AS) vX.Y**

### 3.3 Core + Axioms
- **Structural Coherence — Core Specification (SC-CORE-<ID>) vX.Y**
- **Structural Coherence — Axioms Specification (SC-AXIOM-<ID>) vX.Y**

### 3.4 Downstream Artifacts
Recommended prefixes:
- **SC-PROFILE-<ID> vX.Y**
- **SC-EXT-<ID> vX.Y**
- **SC-CONF-<ID> vX.Y** (conformance tests/suites/procedures)
- **SC-IMPL-<ID> vX.Y** (optional internal tracking; public implementations may use their own names)

### 3.5 Governance / Program Docs (Non-AS)
Recommended prefixes:
- **SC-GOV-<ID> vX.Y**
- **SC-CERT-<ID> vX.Y** (certification program rules, mark usage, appeals, revocation)

---

## 4. Allowed Claims and Required Language (Normative)

### 4.1 Compatibility / Implementation Claim
Implementers MAY truthfully claim:
- "Implements **SC-AS vX.Y**"
- "Compatible with **SC-AS vX.Y**"
- "Implements **SC-AS vX.Y** + **SC-PROFILE-<ID> vA.B**"

Implementers MUST specify the referenced AS version when making compatibility claims.

### 4.2 No Misrepresentation of the AS
Downstream Artifacts and Implementations MUST NOT be presented as the AS.

### 4.3 Certification Claims
Implementers MUST NOT claim certification unless explicitly certified under the published certification program.

Required language:
- Use "Certified Conformance" only if certified.
- Use certification marks only as permitted in the Trademark & Certification Mark Policy.

---

## 5. Licensing and Marks Separation (Normative Doctrine)

### 5.1 AS Immutability
The AS is distributed as an immutable reference artifact. It may be redistributed only in unmodified form, with provenance intact, as governed by the AS license.

### 5.2 Post-AS Implementation Freedom
Downstream implementations and derivatives are **free to implement**, including commercially, provided:
- the AS is not modified or misrepresented as AS, and
- no certification is claimed without authorization.

**Canonical statement:**
> Post-AS implementation is free (including commercial) provided no certification is claimed and the AS is not modified or misrepresented.

### 5.3 Certification is Mark-Based
Certification is controlled via trademarks/certification marks and the published certification program—not by restricting the ability to implement.

**Canonical statement:**
> Certification is a trademark/certification-mark claim, not a permission to implement.

---

## 6. Citations and Versioning (Normative)

When referencing the AS in any document, publication, or product claim, use this format:

- **Structural Coherence — Anchor Specification (SC-AS) vX.Y**
- Include the release identifier(s) used by Coherence Research (e.g., document IDs and/or cryptographic anchors) where applicable.

Examples:
- "This Profile references **SC-AS v1.0**."
- "This system implements **SC-AS v1.0**."
- "This system achieved **Certified Conformance** for **SC-AS v1.0**."

---

## 7. Non-Normative Notes

- The term "Canon" is replaced by **Anchor Specification (AS)** to improve legibility and standards-body neutrality.
- Governance, certification, and administrative documentation should be clearly labeled as non-AS unless explicitly included in an AS release.
